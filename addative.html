Additive Manufacturing

Modern 3D printing has become very accessible to hobbyists and professionals. Most 3D printers come with installation instructions offering a plug and play system. Oftentimes coming pre-calibrated, these machines can print complex models straight out of the box. In addition, user-friendly slicing programs allow users to simply import a 3D model, adjust a few settings, and send it directly to print with minimal technical knowledge.

There are online communities and libraries of models which have made it easier than ever to get started without needing advanced printing skills. Platforms like Thingiverse offer thousands of premade files that users can print immediately or customize to fit their needs. The extra accessibility allows beginners to tune their printer how they like, experimenting with different materials and settings with minimal effort. In addition, community forums and videos provide tutorials and troubleshooting advice for users looking to improve their skills over time.

Ultimaker Cura offers one of the best free slicing software packages. Perfect for hobbyists just getting into 3D printing.

Recommendations

Creality K2

Great print quality of of the box. Very easy for beginners to use.

Creality Ender 3 V2

Affordable 3D printer for hardcore hobbyists. Open source firmware and hardware customization.

Bambu Labs P2S

Middle tier 3D printer offering ease of use for advanced hobbyists.

UltiMaker S5

Top tier 3D printer offering ease of use and extreme reliability for companies.
